The Bucket List.

Lions Road. Border Ranges. The Old Glen Innes Road. Four days, twelve hundred kilometres, one convict tunnel — the trip every Brisbane adventure rider should do once.

4 days 1,200 km Intermediate + Apr — Oct

South of the QLD border, the country changes character. The forests deepen, the gravel reddens, and the roads acquire history — convict tunnels, ghost towns, river crossings cut into granite. This article is one trip in four days.

The Brisbane ADV bucket list isn't a list. It's a loop. Lions Road south to Kyogle. Border Ranges scenic drive. Tabulam to Tenterfield. The Old Glen Innes Road — 160 km of mostly dirt cutting through national park to a 20-metre hand-cut convict tunnel, then down to Grafton. Casino and Kyogle for the return. Twelve hundred kilometres in four days, almost half of it unsealed, no day longer than 350 km.

What sets this loop apart from the Article II destinations is not the difficulty. It's the character. You are riding through a landscape that was a major freight corridor from the 1840s to the 1930s and has been quietly reverting to wilderness ever since. The Old Glen Innes Road follows the Boyd River. It passes Dalmorton, which was once a town. The convict tunnel is cut by hand. And on a good Tenere 700 with K60s, all of it is yours.

Caveat — registration The NSW NPWS and Forestry Corporation enforce vehicle registration aggressively in this country. Conditional / recreational rego is not legal on these roads. If you're caught on a non-road-registered bike, you'll be fined and your bike may be impounded. The cost-saving is not worth it.
The Loop — 4 days · 1,200 km · ~50% unsealed

Schematic route plan — see the day-by-day breakdown below for fuel stops, accommodation, and surface notes.

Day One · ≈ 300 km · 50% unsealed

Brisbane → Tenterfield

via Rathdowney, Lions Road & Tabulam
Overnight Tenterfield · Royal Hotel
Riding time ~6 hr · stops, fuel, photos
PLATE 10 / LIONS ROAD
S 28.32° / E 152.96° The Lions Road — Rathdowney to Wiangaree

The Lions Road is a 96 km sealed mountain shortcut linking Rathdowney (QLD) to Kyogle (NSW). Maintained by donations from the local Lions Club — there's a box at the border.

Day One is mostly tarmac, on purpose. You're warming up for the harder days and you're crossing the border. Out of Brisbane on the Mt Lindesay Highway to Rathdowney; right at the pub onto the Lions Road; over the McPherson Range to Kyogle. The Lions Road is famous for its sweepers and was the site of the (now-discontinued) Lions TT motorcycle festival. The road remains open as a public road and is excellent.

From Kyogle, take the slower, twistier western route via Bonalbo to Tabulam. This is where the dirt starts. Tabulam to Tenterfield via Drake gives you the first proper New England gravel. Tenterfield itself is a frontier town with a half-decent pub, good coffee, and the Royal Hotel for the night.

Logistics

Fuel
Beaudesert (or directly Rathdowney). Kyogle. Tabulam. Tenterfield. No long gaps.
Lunch
Kyogle bakery, or push to Tabulam Hotel.
Accommodation
Royal Hotel Tenterfield, Stannum House, Tenterfield Lodge. Book ahead in the cool months.
Notes
You'll cross the QLD–NSW border on the Lions Road. Drop the donation in the box at the top — it pays for road maintenance.
Day Two · ≈ 280 km · 80% unsealed

Tenterfield → Grafton

The Old Glen Innes Road
Overnight Grafton
Riding time ~7 hr · take your time
PLATE 11 / OLD GLEN INNES RD
S 29.65° / E 152.40° Old Glen Innes Road — Boyd River country

"Dirt for almost its entire length… narrow and tricky one-lane track was the only road link between New England towns and the NSW coast for close to a century from the 1840s." — Practical Motoring.

Day Two is the day. The Old Glen Innes Road is the reason you came.

From Tenterfield, drop south to Glen Innes (sealed, fast, get it done). Refuel. Then turn east for the Old Glen Innes Road, which is graded gravel for almost the entire 160 km to Grafton. It cuts through Washpool and Gibraltar Range national parks, follows the Boyd River, passes the site of Dalmorton — once a town, now a ghost — and runs through a hand-cut convict tunnel about 20 metres long that you'll ride through with a torch. This road was the only freight link from New England to the NSW coast for almost a century. It now sees one or two cars per hour.

The ride is well within reach of a Tenere 700 / 890 on Heidenau K60s or a GSA on dirt-biased tyres. There are no technical climbs, but the surface has loose gravel in places and concentration matters. Long. Quiet. Beautiful. Bring spare fuel if you're on a small-tank bike.

Logistics

Critical fuel note
No fuel between Glen Innes and Grafton — ~160 km of mostly dirt. Top up at Glen Innes. If you're on a DR650 or DR-Z400, carry a 4 L Rotopax or stop at Newton Boyd if it's open.
Pace
Average 40-50 km/h moving. Plan ~5 hours of riding plus stops. Bring lunch.
The tunnel
Near Dalmorton. Hand-cut, ~20 m long. Carry a headtorch — your high beam helps, but it's properly dark.
Accommodation
Grafton has plenty — Grafton Hotel, Roches Hotel, various motels. Bookings advisable in summer.
Weather
This road becomes seriously slippery after rain. If > 30 mm has fallen in the past 48 hours, consider the alternative tar route via Casino.
Day Three · ≈ 240 km · 30% unsealed

Grafton → Kyogle

via Mann River, Casino & the Richmond Range
Overnight Kyogle (or push to Murwillumbah)
Riding time ~4 hr · easy day

Day Three is your recovery day. North out of Grafton along the Clarence River, then west on the Mann River Nature Reserve back-roads — a beautiful, lightly trafficked riverside ride with a few gravel sections. Casino for lunch (the Saleyards Café is excellent). Onward to Kyogle via the Richmond Range, which has good unsealed connectors if you want a longer day. Stop at the Kyogle Bakery for the legendary meat pies.

If you're feeling fresh, push on to Murwillumbah and stay at the Imperial Hotel for an extra easy run home tomorrow. Otherwise, Kyogle has the Kyogle Hotel, the Exchange Hotel, and the Kyogle Cabins on the way out of town.

Logistics

Fuel
Grafton, Casino, Kyogle. No gaps.
Lunch
Casino Saleyards Café or the Commercial Hotel.
Accommodation
Kyogle Hotel, Exchange Hotel, Kyogle Caravan Park.
Day Four · ≈ 350 km · 60% unsealed

Kyogle → Brisbane

via Border Ranges Tweed Range Scenic Drive
Final leg The crown jewel of the loop
Riding time ~7 hr · plus the Tweed Range
PLATE 12 / BORDER RANGES
S 28.43° / E 153.04° Tweed Range Scenic Drive · Border Ranges NP

"All the roads through the Border Ranges are unsealed though generally in very good condition and easily manageable in a 2WD." — The Sandy Feet. The southern descent to Lillian Rock is the loose-gravel exception.

Day Four delivers the loop's second showpiece: the Tweed Range Scenic Drive, a 64 km unsealed loop through Border Ranges National Park. Per NPWS: "the only access road, traversing the park from west to south in a boomerang-like arc." It connects Sheep Station Creek and Forest Tops camp areas, climbs to lookouts over the Mt Warning caldera, and drops back down to Lillian Rock for the slipperiest descent of the entire trip — large, loose gravel that has caught out many a confident rider on dual-sport tyres.

From the Tweed Range, descend to Murwillumbah, cross the border at Tweed Heads, and ride the Pacific Motorway home. Or — if you have one more day in you — take the slower route via the Numinbah Valley and the Gold Coast Hinterland.

NSW NPWS fee Border Ranges National Park charges $8 per vehicle per day. Exact cash in envelopes at park entrances. The NSW NPWS Multi Park Pass (annual) is the better value if you'll be in NSW national parks more than four times a year.

Logistics

Fee
$8 per vehicle, exact cash, envelope system.
Camping (alternative to driving home)
Sheep Station Creek and Forest Tops camp areas in Border Ranges NP. Book via nationalparks.nsw.gov.au. Spectacular sites.
Hazard
The Lillian Rock descent — loose, large gravel. Brake early, stand on the pegs, weight back. Standard ADV protocol but more important here than on most of the loop.
Final fuel
Kyogle. Murwillumbah. Then home.
EXPANSION — THE NEW ENGLAND ADDITION

If you have a fifth or sixth day to spare, drop the route from Tenterfield down through the New England High Country: Glen Innes, Armidale, Walcha. This is the country covered by the HEMA New England High Country NSW Motorcycle Touring map, produced with BMW Motorrad Australia specifically for motorcyclists. It is the single best paper reference for the NSW side of any Brisbane ADV trip. Buy it before you go.

Other classic roads in the New England area include Thunderbolt's Way (Walcha to Gloucester, mostly sealed, dramatic descent through Carson's Pioneer Lookout), Waterfall Way (Armidale to Coffs Harbour, sealed, the most spectacular sealed motorcycle road in eastern Australia), the Bruxner Highway, and the Oxley Highway (Walcha to Wauchope — sealed, but consistently rated one of Australia's top three motorcycle roads).

Variety Adventure Ride The annual Variety Adventure Ride charity event follows a roughly Tamworth → Tenterfield → Casino → Ballina → Glen Innes loop of about 1,780 km over six days. Worth diarising as a more structured option if a self-organised four-day loop feels intimidating. Details at amcn.com.au.
A NOTE ON THE BICENTENNIAL NATIONAL TRAIL

The Bicentennial National Trail is a 5,330 km horse-and-walker trail from Cooktown to Healesville that passes through six of the regions in this atlas. It is not open to motorbikes. Per the National Trail: "The Trail is not open to motorised vehicles or trail bikes, and pets are not permitted." Some forum threads on advrider.com discuss whether sections might be rideable; the answer is no. What ADV riders do is ride the parallel gravel back-roads that shadow the BNT corridor through Blackbutt, Ravensbourne, Crows Nest, Killarney and beyond. Many of those roads appear in this atlas.
