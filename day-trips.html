Day Trips.

Home before dinner. Three ride zones inside a two-hour radius — the half-day classics that earn your bike its first scratches.

Sub-2 hr drive 120 – 250 km loops All skill levels Year-round

The first thing every new Brisbane adventure rider needs is a Sunday morning loop they can finish before lunch. The second thing they need is permission to fall in love with it — to ride it ten times before moving on.

Brisbane has three home-range zones that meet this brief. Mt Mee in the northern half of D'Aguilar National Park; the gravel-and-pine plantation network of Beerburrum and Beerwah State Forests; and the back-road grid south of Boonah that links the Scenic Rim's quietest farmland to Mt Barney's foothills. All three sit inside a 90-minute ride from the CBD. All three are legal to ride on a registered bike, with no permit and no booking. And all three pass at least one bakery that does a proper steak-and-onion roll.

Before you ride Trail bikes in QLD national parks and state forests must be fully road-registered; conditional / recreational registration is explicitly excluded. Your motorcycle licence must be valid. Normal road rules apply on every forest road. Detail in Article IV.

What follows are the three destinations in order of distance from Brisbane CBD, with everything you need to plan a Saturday: where to enter, what the surface is doing, where the fuel stops are, and which café to use as the meeting point.

Three home-range zones — within 90 min of the CBD

Schematic — three home-range loops radiating from Brisbane. No. 01 Mt Mee (via Dayboro → Gantry → Woodford), No. 02 Beerburrum / Glasshouse (via the Bruce → plantation → Maleny), No. 03 Scenic Rim (via Beaudesert → Rathdowney → Boonah).

Destination No. 01

Mt Mee

D'Aguilar National Park, northern section
Co-ordinates S 27° 04′   E 152° 44′
Distance from CBD 70 km · 60 min
PLATE 01 / MT MEE
S 27.07° / E 152.74° Western Escarpment forest drive

The western escarpment falls away toward Moreton Bay. On a clear winter morning you can see Bribie Island from the Gantry. Range Road runs along this ridge for 12 km.

Surface Gravel forest drives, creek crossings
Difficulty Beginner
Bike suitability Lightweight dual-sport → heavyweight adventure
Permit / fee None

Mt Mee is the easiest entry point in this atlas: a 70 km ride from inner Brisbane to a network of signed, gazetted forest drives — Western Escarpment, Mount Mee, and Range Road — that twist through pine plantation and eucalypt forest to the Moreton Bay rim. Most riders enter via Dayboro, climbing Mount Mee Road (Tourist Route 29) and turning into Sellin Road to reach The Gantry, the trail-bike-friendly day-use area that serves as the de-facto staging post.

From The Gantry, the loop runs north along Range Road to Somerset Lookout, drops into the Neurum Creek catchment, then climbs back out via Jacky Creek and Rasmussen Road to Woodford. Round-trip from Brisbane, it's a comfortable 200 km with roughly 30 per cent dirt — a perfect first-week outing on a new adventure bike.

Mount Mee access roads will be closed following a rain event of greater than 100 mm within a 24-hour period due to causeway flooding.

That's QPWS speaking. Heed it. The escarpment causeways flood fast and stay closed for 48 hours afterward. In the wet season — December through February — check the parks.qld.gov.au alerts page before leaving Brisbane. Out of the wet, Mt Mee is rideable virtually year-round.

Logistics

Staging
BP Carseldine or directly at Dayboro for a 7:00 am roll-out. The Adventure Riders Brisbane group meets at Dayboro most cool-season Sundays.
Fuel
Top up at Dayboro on the way in; Woodford on the way out. Don't rely on Mt Mee village.
Camping
Neurum Creek and Archer camping areas inside the Mt Mee section. $7.50 pppn, book online via book.parks.qld.gov.au.
Coffee & lunch
CJ's Patisserie, Woodford. The unofficial post-ride canteen for SEQ adventure riders.
Hazards
Shared with 4WDs, walkers, horse riders. The locked gate between the Mt Mee and southern D'Aguilar sections frequently blocks through-traffic — plan to loop back out via Woodford.
Note — southern D'Aguilar Mt Nebo and Mt Glorious are the famous tarmac roads west of Brisbane, but the southern fire-road network there is closed to trail bikes. It's walking, horse and mountain bike country only. The Mt Mee section is the only D'Aguilar zone open to adventure motorcycles.
Destination No. 02

Beerburrum & Beerwah

State forests, Glasshouse Mountains
Co-ordinates S 26° 52′   E 152° 57′
Distance from CBD 70 km · 60 min
PLATE 02 / BEERBURRUM
S 26.87° / E 152.96° Hoop pine plantation

Hoop pine plantation south of the Glasshouse Mountains. Formed gravel roads run between the plantations in a near-grid; perfect for first rides standing on the pegs.

Surface Flat gravel, plantation roads
Difficulty Beginner
Bike suitability Any registered bike
Permit / fee Solo: none · Groups 5+: HQP permit

Beerburrum and Beerwah are HQPlantations-managed hoop pine plantations south and east of the Glasshouse Mountains. The riding here is flat, fast, and forgiving: a grid of formed gravel roads winding between rows of planted hoop pine, broken occasionally by the volcanic plug silhouettes that give the area its name. It's the place to take a friend who has just bought their first ADV bike and never ridden off-pavement.

Access is straight off the Bruce Highway: exit at Beerburrum or Beerwah and follow Roys Road or Old Gympie Road into the plantation. Daylight hours only. The cardinal rule is to stay on formed roads — those marked by paired blue tree blazes are fire tracks, not public roads, and not for you, permit or not.

If you only do one beginner loop — Brisbane → Roys Road → through the plantation → Glasshouse Mountains Lookout → Maleny via Mount Mellum Road → home via Landsborough. Approximately 220 km round-trip. About 40 per cent dirt. The Glasshouse views alone earn it.

Logistics

Permits
No individual permit. Organised motorised groups of 5+ riders need an HQP organised-event permit — apply via hqplantations.com.au.
Hours
Daylight only. Strictly enforced.
Other users
4WDs, mountain bikers, horse riders. Logging trucks on weekdays.
Fuel
Beerburrum, Beerwah and Landsborough all have servos. Plenty of options.
Coffee
The Beerwah Hotel, Beerburrum Bakery, or push on to Maleny for the cool-climate cafés.
Destination No. 03

The Scenic Rim

Rathdowney & Mt Barney foothills
Co-ordinates S 28° 13′   E 152° 53′
Distance from CBD 110 km · 90 min
PLATE 03 / SCENIC RIM
S 28.21° / E 152.88° Mt Barney from Carneys Creek Road

Mt Barney rises behind the gravel back-roads south of Boonah. The Carneys Creek loop is the unofficial first big-bike test of the Brisbane ADV calendar.

Surface Gravel back-roads, some 4WD tracks
Difficulty Beginner → Intermediate
Bike suitability Lightweight dual-sport → mid-weight adventure
Permit / fee Roads: none · Adventure Park: paid entry

Head south from Brisbane on the Mt Lindesay Highway, turn at Rathdowney, and you enter the Scenic Rim — the lush, sub-tropical horseshoe of mountains that forms the QLD–NSW border. The ride here is mostly the gravel between the famous peaks: Carneys Creek Road, Cannon Creek Road, the Croftby back-loop, the climb up to Spicers Gap. None of it is technical, but it puts you within view of Mt Barney, Mt Maroon, and the cliffs of the Border Ranges for hours at a stretch.

The optional add-on is Scenic Rim Adventure Park, a privately-managed 695-acre property near Rathdowney with 28 km of 4WD tracks, the gentler sections of which suit a mid-size ADV bike. Day-pass entry, hot showers at the gate, on-site bush camping. It's where you go when you've done Mt Mee a dozen times and want a graduation step.

Pub stop Rathdowney Hotel — long-standing rider stop, generous parking out the front. Rosevale Retreat, on the way home, is the state's oldest hotel building (1852) per Wikipedia — closed since 2015, but a worthwhile photo-stop on the BNT corridor for the historically inclined.

Logistics

Staging
Boonah is the natural gateway. Fuel at the BP on the highway, then turn south for Rathdowney.
Fuel
Boonah, Rathdowney, Beaudesert. The full loop has no fuel gaps over ~60 km.
Adventure Park entry
Day pass via visitscenicrim.com.au. Hot showers, bush camping, dump point.
The loop
Boonah → Carneys Creek Road → Mt Lindesay Hwy → Rathdowney → Croftby → Boonah. About 130 km, 60 per cent unsealed.
DAY TRIPS AT A GLANCE

Three home-range zones. Pick by mood and skill.

Easiest first ride
Beerburrum — flat gravel, plenty of escape routes, no creek crossings.
Best Sunday loop
Mt Mee — Brisbane → Dayboro → The Gantry → Woodford → home. CJ's Patisserie at the end.
Most scenic
The Scenic Rim — Mt Barney in your peripheral vision the entire time.
Most varied
Scenic Rim Adventure Park — 28 km of tracks from easy to genuinely 4WD-grade.
When to avoid
Mt Mee after > 100 mm rain (causeways close). Beerburrum after dark (gates close). Scenic Rim back-roads in school holidays (4WD traffic).
