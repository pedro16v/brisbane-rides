A NOTE FROM THE EDITORS
Why this exists

Brisbane is one of the easiest capitals in Australia for a weekend dual-sport ride.

And yet — search "dirt trails near Brisbane" and the first thing you'll find is the Council telling you they're banned in every park within the city limits.

The story doesn't end there. Drive ninety minutes in any direction and you cross into a country of forestry roads: gazetted public roads cutting through state forests and national parks, legal to ride on a registered bike, lightly trafficked, and stitched together into loops that rival anywhere on the continent. This atlas is for the rider who has bought their first adventure bike, joined the Adventure Riders Brisbane Facebook group, and wants to know where to go on Saturday morning.

The fifteen destinations within are organised by how much weekend they demand: a Sunday morning, an overnighter, or the bucket-list four-day loop. Each entry tells you the legalities, the camp fees, how much fuel to carry, whether to ride in or trailer, and which pub to stop at on the way home. None of it is paywalled. All of it is current to autumn 2026.
